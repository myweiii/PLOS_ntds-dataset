The gastrointestinal involvement of Chagas disease is a neglected manifestation of a neglected disease. Despite this, it causes immense suffering to those affected, which lasts for years. The diagnosis is usually late and the treatment is difficult to access and inadequate, causing the patient to be operated on an emergency basis with major complications and, consequently, high mortality. Here we present the epidemiological profile and costs of public and government-funded hospitalizations, using the Brazilian national database.
