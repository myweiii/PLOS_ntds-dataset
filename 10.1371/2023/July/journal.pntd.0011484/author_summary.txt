This study has appreciably indicated that the prevalence of soil-transmitted helminthes are association with water, sanitation, and hygiene factors that were mainly affected the school-age children as they are a highly vulnerable group of the community especially within the city setups. This is valuable to health and education sectors to direct planning and resources to target the common health problems relating to the soil-transmitted helminthes and water, sanitation, and hygiene factors. It also gives insights about the current situations of the school environment as well as corrective interventions that will be implemented for children infected with STH. Further, the finding contributes to strengthening health-promoting behaviors.
